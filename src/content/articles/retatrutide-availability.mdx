## Quick Answer

Retatrutide is not available as an FDA-approved prescription product. It is available in clinical research settings through official studies, and it is also listed by research vendors for research use.

That distinction is the whole availability story. Clinical-trial availability depends on eligibility and site location. Research-vendor availability depends on vendor listings, documentation, pricing, payment support, and shipping. Future prescription availability depends on Lilly's clinical program and regulatory review.

## What Availability Means

Retatrutide availability can mean three different things:

| Availability type | What it means |
|---|---|
| Clinical trial access | Official study participation through a trial site and screening process. |
| Research vendor listings | Research-use listings from peptide vendors, with quality signals to compare. |
| Prescription availability | Future regulated access if retatrutide is approved. |

Most confusion comes from mixing those categories. A vendor listing does not mean FDA approval, and a clinical-trial listing does not mean open access for everyone.

The fastest way to answer an availability question is to ask what kind of access the person actually means. If they mean prescription access, the answer is no. If they mean trial access, the answer depends on location and eligibility. If they mean research-vendor availability, the answer is a comparison problem: which listings are visible, documented, and practical to evaluate?

## Clinical Development Progress

Retatrutide has strong clinical momentum. Lilly has reported Phase 3 results from TRIUMPH-4, where the 12 mg group achieved 28.7% mean weight loss at 68 weeks in adults with obesity or overweight and knee osteoarthritis. Lilly has also reported TRANSCEND-T2D-1 results in type 2 diabetes, including A1C reductions and weight loss.

Those updates make future availability a serious question. The compound is no longer just a mechanism story; it has later-stage clinical data behind it. Still, official prescription availability depends on the full development and review process.
