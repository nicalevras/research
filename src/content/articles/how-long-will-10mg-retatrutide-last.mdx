## Quick Answer

How long 10 mg of retatrutide lasts depends on the selected research amount. The basic math is total vial amount divided by the amount used in each research interval.

BAC water does not change how much retatrutide is in the vial. It changes concentration. A 10 mg vial mixed with 1 mL and a 10 mg vial mixed with 2 mL still both contain 10 mg total.

## The Core Calculation

The vial-duration calculation is simple:

| Step | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Start with total amount | A 10 mg vial contains 10 mg total. |
| Choose the research amount | This is the amount being modeled in the calculation. |
| Divide total by amount | 10 mg divided by the selected amount gives the number of intervals. |

For example, if a research calculation uses 1 mg per interval, 10 mg equals 10 intervals. If the calculation uses 2 mg per interval, 10 mg equals 5 intervals. These are math examples, not dosing instructions.

This is the cleanest way to avoid confusion: 10 mg is the inventory. The research amount is the drawdown. The number of intervals is inventory divided by drawdown.

## BAC Water Does Not Change Total Amount

BAC water changes concentration. It does not create more retatrutide and it does not make the vial last longer by itself.

| 10 mg vial mixed with | Concentration | Total retatrutide |
|---|---:|---:|
| 1 mL | 10 mg/mL | 10 mg |
| 2 mL | 5 mg/mL | 10 mg |
| 2.5 mL | 4 mg/mL | 10 mg |
| 3 mL | 3.33 mg/mL | 10 mg |

The final liquid volume changes, but the total peptide amount does not.

## Vial Duration Versus Half-Life

"How long will 10 mg last" is not a half-life question. Half-life describes how exposure changes in the body over time. Vial duration describes how many research calculations fit into a fixed vial amount.

That distinction matters because retatrutide is studied as a once-weekly compound in trials, but a vial-duration calculation is still just amount math.

People often mix these ideas because both use the phrase "how long." In pharmacology, "how long" means exposure over time. In vial math, "how long" means how many calculated intervals fit in a vial. Those questions need different answers.

For pharmacology context, see [retatrutide half-life](/articles/retatrutide-half-life).

## FAQ

### How long does 10mg retatrutide last?

It depends on the selected research amount. Divide 10 mg by that amount to estimate the number of intervals.

### Does adding more BAC water make 10mg last longer?

No. More BAC water lowers concentration but does not increase the total amount of retatrutide.

### Is 10mg the same as a clinical dose?

No. A vial amount is a product amount. A clinical dose is part of a study protocol.

### Should I use half-life to calculate vial duration?

No. Half-life and vial duration are different concepts.
