## Quick Answer

GHK-Cu peptide injection dosage is a concentration problem before it is a number problem. A copied amount is not useful unless you know the vial size, how much liquid was added, and what syringe scale is being used.

This matters because GHK-Cu is a high-interest peptide. The appeal is skin quality, collagen, repair signaling, recovery appearance, and hair/scalp research, so the math deserves the same seriousness as the benefit claims.

## Injection Dosage Starts With The Vial

A lyophilized GHK-Cu vial is usually labeled by total peptide amount. Common search terms mention 50 mg and 100 mg because those vial sizes show up often in the research market.

The label alone does not tell you the final concentration. A 50 mg vial mixed with 1 mL is not the same as a 50 mg vial mixed with 5 mL. Once liquid is added, concentration changes.

| Vial detail | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Total milligrams | Sets the total amount available. |
| Reconstitution volume | Controls concentration. |
| Target amount | Determines draw volume. |
| Syringe type | Determines how the volume appears as units. |
